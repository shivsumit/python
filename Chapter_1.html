1. Chemical Equations

üîç Definition

A chemical equation is a symbolic representation of a chemical reaction. It shows the reactants (starting materials) and products (substances formed) using chemical formulas and symbols.

üí° Example

Magnesium burns in air to form magnesium oxide:

Reactants ‚Üí Products

üìå Key Points

  • Arrow (‚Üí) separates reactants from products
  • Plus sign (+) separates multiple reactants or products
  • Chemical formulas represent compounds
  • Symbols represent elements

2. Balanced Chemical Equations

üîç Definition

A balanced chemical equation has equal numbers of atoms of each element on both sides of the equation. This follows the Law of Conservation of Mass.

‚öñÔ∏è Law of Conservation of Mass

"Mass can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction."

Therefore: Mass of reactants = Mass of products

üí° Balancing Example

Unbalanced equation:

Mg + O‚ÇÇ ‚Üí MgO

Balanced equation:

2Mg + O‚ÇÇ ‚Üí 2MgO

üéØ Steps to Balance Equations

  1. Count atoms of each element on both sides
  2. Start with the most complex molecule
  3. Use coefficients (not subscripts) to balance
  4. Balance metals first, then non-metals, then hydrogen and oxygen
  5. Check that all atoms are balanced

Corrosion is the process where metals are slowly eaten away by the action of air, moisture, or chemicals.

4.2 Rancidity

Rancidity is the condition when fats and oils are oxidized and they become rancid. This gives unpleasant smell and taste to food.

Prevention Methods:
  • ‚Ä¢ Antioxidants: Adding BHT, BHA
  • ‚Ä¢ Vacuum packing: Removing air
  • ‚Ä¢ Nitrogen flushing: Replacing air with nitrogen
  • ‚Ä¢ Refrigeration: Storing at low temperatures
  • ‚Ä¢ Airtight containers: Preventing contact with air
